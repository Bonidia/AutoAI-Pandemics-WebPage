Regulations and Submission Guidelines

Deadline: April 24, 2024


Annual Theme for 2024

AI Innovations for Pandemic and Epidemic Preparedness and Response in the Global South


Objectives

Technological advancements in Artificial Intelligence (AI) have benefited our society and economy. However, AI development has been concentrated in technologically advanced countries, often overlooking the unique needs and challenges faced by less developed regions, such as the Global South. Therefore, the I Global South Olympiad for Good aims to harness AI to address critical social issues in the Global South, emphasizing projects that offer free access to communities in need.


Eligibility

Open to any individual or team from the Global South currently residing there. The focus is on non-commercial, open-access projects.


Regions Eligible for Competition

This competition is open to participants from specified regions in the Global South, aiming to address and solve local challenges through AI. Below are the regions eligible for this competition:

  • Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Latin America and the Caribbean
  • South and Southeast Asia
  • Central Asia
  • The Middle East and North Africa (MENA)
  • Oceania (excluding Australia and New Zealand)

Participants must currently reside in one of the above regions to be eligible for the competition.


Submission Requirements

Participants are required to prepare and submit the following components as part of their competition entry (Text and Presentation must be in English):

  • Project Document: A comprehensive two-page document that includes:
    • An outline of the project concept.
    • A clear articulation of the anticipated impact.
    • A well-structured implementation plan.
    This document should serve as a blueprint, detailing the innovative use of AI.

  • Video Presentation: A video not exceeding 3 minutes in length, covering:
    • The project's vision and its significance in mitigating the impacts of future health crises in the Global South.
    • A succinct overview of the proposed solution's functionality.
    The video is an opportunity to visually and verbally engage the review panel.

All submissions must comprehensively address the above requirements to be considered for the competition.

Competition Structure

Phase 1: Idea Submission

Participants submit their projects by a specified deadline, focusing on innovation, accessibility, and potential social impact.


Phase 2: Regional Selection

Best ideas from each region are selected by a panel of AI experts, focusing on feasibility, innovation, and community impact.


Phase 3: Final Pitch and Development Grant Award

Regional winners present their ideas in a global event. The top projects are awarded a scholarship to develop their idea at the University of São Paulo, Brazil.
